Special Session on Trustworthy and Responsible Data Mining (DMSP 2025)

Kyoto, Japan | 22nd - 24th October, 2025

Abstract

The increasing reliance on data mining techniques across various domains necessitates a strong emphasis on trustworthiness and responsibility...

Background

As data mining becomes an integral part of decision-making in fields such as finance, healthcare, and smart cities, the need for responsible and ethical practices has intensified. The intersection of artificial intelligence and privacy regulations like GDPR and CCPA presents unique challenges that demand innovative solutions. The session will explore the ethical, technical, and regulatory aspects of responsible data mining.

Aims and Scope

  • Fairness-aware data mining techniques
  • Transparency and interpretability in data mining
  • Privacy-preserving data mining methods
  • Secure multi-party computation for data mining
  • Federated learning and differential privacy
  • Adversarial attacks and defenses in data mining
  • Blockchain-based security mechanisms
  • Case studies and practical applications of responsible data mining

Formatting Guidelines

Submissions should follow the Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) format. The page limit for full papers is 12 pages, including references. Authors should ensure their submissions meet the formatting requirements outlined in the official conference guidelines.

Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit original research papers via the conference submission system. Submissions will be peer-reviewed and should not be under consideration elsewhere. Accepted papers will be included in the conference proceedings.

More details on submission procedures can be found at ADMA 2025 Website.
